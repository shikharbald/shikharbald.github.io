Software Engineer · Toronto

Hi, I'm Shikhar.

I build distributed systems and developer tooling. Currently at Stripe, working on the infrastructure that connects banks to the global payments network.

I studied Economics and Computer Science at UBC — an unusual combination I keep finding useful. The econ degree gave me a way of thinking about systems: incentives, constraints, second-order effects. The CS degree gave me the tools to act on it. My undergrad thesis used Poisson regression and ELO ratings to quantify what Messi would be worth in the Premier League — the kind of problem at the intersection of statistics and sport that drew me into quantitative work in the first place.

Before Stripe I spent time at EA building real-time social graph pipelines for 5M+ players, and at Capital One re-architecting APIs and microservices on AWS. I write occasionally about engineering and things I've been figuring out.
